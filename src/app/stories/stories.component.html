Phool Deli "Festival of Spring"

Phool Dei, a common folk festival is celebrated in the state, only to welcome the spring season with enthusiasm and fervor. Villagers, especially children in this festival pluck the first flowers of the season from the meadows and scatter those on the threshold of homes. In some places, the festival is celebrated as carnival and the celebration goes on for a month. Basanti, folk songs are composed for the coming spring season when flowers bloom and new life spring in the valleys of the hills of Garhwal. The folk song is sung individually or in groups.

Uttarakhand as a Sainik Dham

Uttarakhand is home of Char Dhams of "Yamunotri", "Gangotri","Kedarnath" and "Badrinath". Prime Minister of India Shri Narendra Modi called Uttarakhand a Sinik Dham.With this Uttarakhand government announced to develop Sainik Dham as a fifth dham in state. This is to recognize the unique contribution Uttarakhand in security of the country and leadership role played by people of state in armed forces of the country.It is the home of the headquaters of three regiments/ Rifles of Indian Army viz Kumaun Regiment,Garhwal Rifles at Lansdowne.

Khatrua Festival

Khatrua is a festival of Pastoral and agricultural Society,which is celebrated on the first day of month of Ashwin mid of September and it specifies beginning of Autumn Season.People prepare bonfires and decorate an tree with flowers,children dance around the bonfire holding flags. Locals take special care of their animals, worship them feed them fresh grass."Cucumber" is served to the fire which is means it will destroy evil influences and remaining cucumber is distributed among people as sacrament.Basically, it is about thanking God for his blessings on the people and their pastures.

Social status of Women in Uttarakhand

Uttarakhand society has always kept women high in regards,women play a dominant role in family,social culture, and economic life. Rani Karanwati and Veer Bala Teelu Rautela are two examples who led from the front when male members of family attained martydom. Similarly, Tinchari Mai ked movement against the intoxication and liquor in the hills.During freedom movement women became equal partners in struggle. Uttarakhand has no history of women subjugation like customs of Sati, Pardah and even widow marriage were also allowed in the society.

Chipko Movement led by Gaura Devi with a group of women to save trees can be seen as the biggest example of leadership. Women lead various struggles against social evils, forest rights etc.

Raj Bhawan Nainital

Raj Bhawan, also called as Governor House is a British era building, now serves as the residence of Governor of Uttarakhand. Known for its magnificent looks and old age charming architecture, Raj Bhawan in Nainital previously served as summer residence of British Governors. Governor House or Raj Bhawan is located in splendid landscape and spreads across vast area. It is a two storied building consisting of 113 rooms used by Britishers as summer residence. British VIP's and guests stayed at Raj Bhawan during their visit to Nainital. It is built in Victoria Gothic style as a replica of Buckingham Palace. Best time to visit Raj Bhawan is in March,May, June,September,October,November,December.
