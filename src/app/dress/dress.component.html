Pahaadi Sanskr̥tiḥ(संस्कृति)

Culture ( संस्कृति [sanskr̥tiḥ]) encompasses religion, food, what we wear, how we wear it, our language, marriage, music, what we believe is right or wrong.

Rangaai Pichora

Pichora is a symbol of the synergy of a married woman and is worn on festivals and social ceremonies and religious occasions according to the prevailing tradition. On the special celebratory occasions like marriage, naming ceremony, fasts, festivals, worship and other important days, women wear it without any restrictions. Pichora is an important part of Uttarakhand’s Kumaon regional marriages.Women wear the Pichora for the first time in their marriage, after which it becomes a ritual for married women to wear it on all special occasions. The center of the Swastik is ‘Om’ which has great importance in meditation and spirituality.

Ghagari For Women

There is a long skirt known as Ghagri which is worn with a choli and Orni. Both Kumaoni and Garhwali women wear this dress. The customary bridal dress of the locals is Ghaghra Lehenga and choli, and Pichora Ghaghra. Married women put on a black necklace i.e. Chareu. Hansuli is a beautiful ornament, a type of necklace, designed in pure gold or silver.Ethnic people cover themselves from Sarong, a mantle-type dress, tightened with a blouse. Rangwalis or Muslim veils are popular costumes in Uttarakhand. They are available in a variety of colors and a holy acquaintance of marriage.

Dhoti For Men

Dhoti or Lungi is worn by many men, which is a lower-garment. The upper-garment is Kurta. Men love to wear turban or headgear which is a part of their traditional costume. There is another alternative dress i.e. Kurta-Pajamas for men that is widespread in this part of India. Both men and women wear woolen jackets and sweaters in winter.
