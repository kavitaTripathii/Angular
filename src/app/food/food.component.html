Pahadi Cuisine

Whenever we talk about traditional foods, Uttarakhand has varieties in taste. Traditional food tells about the culture and versatility of a particular place or a region,cuisine of this place is highly nutritious,easy to prepare and appealing at the same time.It highlights the unique facinating culture ,climate effects on the livings and habits of the locals.This food is very healthy and provides essential nutritions for the high and cold region.

As Uttarakhand is the combination of Kumaun and Gharwal regions receipes can be divided in toe categories: Kumauni Receipes: Bhhang Ki Chutney, Singhal,Sahi and many more. Gharwali Receipes: Roat,Til ki Chatani, Aloo Tamatar Jhol.

Urad Daal Ke Bade

Urad daal ke bade(Wada) are also called pakoras. These are special wadas prepared during festivals like bhai dooj, govardhan pooja , mostly on festive occassions. Receipe for the prepartion of these Wadas is easy so people like to eat in snacks also. It is prepared by soaking Urad daal overnight ,it is starined and grind into fine paste.Then ajwain,garam masala ,heeng and salt are added to that paste. Then small balls are prepared from the paste by applying water on the palms.Then they are deep fried in the sarson oil and are served with chutney.

Bhatt Ki Churdkani

Bhatt is a variety of pulses.Having rich aroma that spreads in the room for a while, this dish is one of the best dish that Uttrakhand's locals eat during winters.It is also spelled as Churkani is a healthy dish and prepared from black bhatt blended with rice paste and flour. Wwith high nutritive value, Bhatt ki Churdkani can be serverd with steamed rice. The dish contains all the essential vitamins, proteins and minerals so people can have it any time.

Mandua Ki Roti

Mandua ki roti is sort of a chapatti(flat bread) prepared with cereal called mundua. It is the part of main course and it served as an option with the normal chapattis made from wheat flour. This roti represents the cooking style of Uttrakhand's locals. It is famous among the tourists visiting Uttarakhand. It is preapred with the mandua flour.

Aloo Tamatar Ki Sabzi

Aloo Tamatar ka Jhol is an easy to cook dish that can be prepared in less than 15 minutes. All you need to do is boil some potatoes, make a curry out of tomatoes and onions, mix both and leave it to cook on low flame. dish is so famous that it is cooked in the same traditional manner in the cities as well as in the villages of Uttarakhand.

Aloo Ke Gutake

This mouthwatering dish comes from the Kumaun region but is equally popular in Gharwal region as well. Every house of Uttarakhand has its unique way tp prepare it and in its every version it tastes amazing. This dish is mostly preapred on special occassions like in fast,weddings,and many rituals. This is served with puri and bhaang ki chutney Kumauni raita.

Dubuk

Having a subtle taste, this dish is good for stomach and tastes better when served with plain white rice, Bhang ki Chutney or Muli Thechwa (crushed radish). Dubuk also referred to as ‘dubuke’ is generally prepared from bhatt ki dal, gahat ki dal or arhar ki dal which is then converted into a fine paste and is cooked on low flame.

Palak Ka Kaapa(Kafuli)

Paalak Ka Kaapa is a thick gravy preparation made from greeny spinach.To prepare this dish, two main ingredients namely spinach and fenugreek leaves are cooked together in an iron pot, after which salt and spices are added to enhance its flavour. The gravy for Kafuli is prepared by making a paste made out of rice or wheat and adding water to it.

Bhaang Ki Chutney

Making our mouth water with its tangy tamarind taste, this famous sauce of Uttarakhand is made from hemp seeds, cumin seeds, garlic leaves, tamarind, and salt. When served with the traditional Pahadi cuisines, it enhances their taste and creates a spell on our taste buds.
